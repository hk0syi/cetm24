üéØ What are Support Vectors?

Support vectors are the critical data points that lie closest to the decision boundary in a Support Vector Machine (SVM). They are the only points that actually determine where the decision boundary is placed.

üîë Key Characteristics

  • Location: They sit exactly on the margin boundaries
  • Importance: Only these points define the decision boundary
  • Efficiency: We only need to store these points for classification
  • Robustness: Other points don't affect the model

Just like a bridge only needs a few strong üèõÔ∏èpillars‚ôñ‚ôñ‚ôñ‚ôñ to stand, an SVM only needs support vectors to define its decision boundary. All other data points are essentially redundant once we have identified the support vectors.

üéì Learning Tip

Remember: Support vectors are called "support" vectors because they literally support the decision boundary - remove them, and the boundary would change. Remove any other points, and the boundary stays the same!
