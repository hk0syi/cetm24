üß™ Isolate mRNA

Extract RNA using solvents or columns, then isolate mRNA specifically using beads with poly-T tails that bind to the poly-A tail of mRNA.

Why mRNA? Because genes are expressed as mRNA - this tells us which genes are actually "switched on"!

Apply the labeled cDNA onto the microarray plate.

Each cDNA molecule binds to its complementary DNA probe spot - like finding the perfect puzzle piece!

5

Use a microarray scanner with a laser. The laser makes the fluorescent tags glow, and a camera records the glowing spots.

This is where biology becomes digital data!

üí° Remember

            
A DNA microarray is like a genetic fingerprinting chip that shows which genes are "switched on" or "off". It's a bridge between the messy, complex world of biology and the clean, comparable world of digital data - enabling scientists to spot patterns, identify disease markers, and understand how treatments affect gene expression.
