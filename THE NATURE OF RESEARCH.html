🎯 Objectivity vs Subjectivity in Research

Objectivity: Being neutral and based on facts, measurements, and observable evidence. It means keeping your personal feelings and opinions out of the research process.
Subjectivity: Letting personal opinions, emotions, or preferences influence the results or observations.
Aspect Objective Subjective
Definition Based on measurable facts Based on personal feelings
Example in Science (Coffee) “The coffee is 70°C and contains 150mg of caffeine.” “This is the best coffee I've ever had!”
Goal in Research To be fair and unbiased Can lead to bias and errors
Used in Scientific experiments, measurements Art, personal opinions, reviews

🎓 In research, we aim for objectivity so our results are accurate, reliable, and trusted by others.
