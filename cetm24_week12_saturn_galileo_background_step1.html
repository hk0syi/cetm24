üåü The Observation Problem

Galileo saw Saturn but couldn't resolve its true shape. The rings appeared as mysterious "ears" or blobs on either side of the planet.

üéØ Not Sampling Variation

The uncertainty wasn't due to random sampling - looking multiple times or having others look wouldn't change what was seen through the limited optics.

üìà Bayesian Solution

Bayesian methods can model the uncertainty and "noise" in observations using probability distributions rather than frequencies.

üí° Key Insight

Bayesian methods don't equate probability with frequency.

In Galileo's case, the uncertainty wasn't about repeated observations giving different results (frequency), but about the inherent limitations and noise in the observation itself. Bayesian analysis can model this type of uncertainty by treating the observation as evidence that updates our beliefs about what Saturn actually looks like.

This approach is now fundamental in modern image analysis, helping us extract meaningful information from noisy or unclear data - from medical imaging to astronomical observations.
