Six Acquaintances & Graph Theory

From Euler’s Bridges of Königsberg to Kevin Bacon

The Small World Property

In 1967, psychologist Stanley Milgram showed that any two people can be connected through a chain of about six acquaintances. This is sometimes called the six degrees of separation.

  • Each person = a vertex (like landmasses in Euler’s bridges).
  • A friendship or collaboration = an edge (like bridges).
  • Finding a path between two people = same concept as path in a graph.

Example Network

In this simplified “Hollywood network”:

  1. You → Friend A → C → Kevin Bacon
  2. You → Friend B → D → Kevin Bacon

Both paths take just 3 steps. In reality, across the whole planet, research shows the number is usually less than six.

From Bridges to Networks

  • Euler’s Königsberg bridges showed how graphs model connectivity problems.
  • The “six acquaintances” phenomenon is the same idea:
    people = nodes, relationships = edges.
  • Social networks demonstrate the power of graph theory everywhere.
