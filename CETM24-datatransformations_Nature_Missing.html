Nature of Missing Values (Income Reporting)

MCAR → No reporting pattern

Meaning: Missingness happens completely by chance.

Income Example: Random loss of survey forms causes some incomes to be missing.

Impact: No bias, only fewer data points.

MAR → Reporting pattern explained by other known data

Meaning: Missingness depends on an observed variable, not the missing value itself.

Income Example: People with lower education are less likely to report income, but within each education group, missingness is unrelated to income.

Impact: Bias can be reduced by adjusting for the observed variable.

MNAR → Reporting pattern tied to the missing value itself

Meaning: Missingness depends directly on the value that is missing.

Income Example: High-income earners are less likely to report income to avoid taxes.

Impact: Causes bias unless the missingness mechanism is modeled.
