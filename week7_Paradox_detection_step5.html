üîç Spurious Correlation

A pattern in data that looks statistically significant but is actually misleading or false because of hidden factors. It's like seeing a mirage in the desert - it looks real, but it's not!

üîÑ Simpson's Paradox

An association that holds in every subgroup can reverse direction when the data are combined. It's like winning every battle but losing the war!

Paradoxes can produce rules that are valid and comprehensible, but actually provide misleading knowledge. Without detection, we risk making wrong decisions based on "fake patterns"!
