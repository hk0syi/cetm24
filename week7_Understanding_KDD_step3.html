KDD is Non-Trivial

Running any data mining algorithm will always produce patterns, but most are obvious, redundant, or trivial.

ü§î Why Non-Trivial?
We must separate useful knowledge from noise through evaluation, filtering, and human judgment‚Äînot just algorithms.

‚ùå Trivial: "Men don't give birth" (obvious)

Patterns must be Valid

Valid means statistically sound, accurate, and not random noise.

üìä Measured by: Support, confidence, accuracy, coverage (objective measures)
PlayTennis Example
Rule: "If Outlook = Overcast ‚Üí PlayTennis = Yes"
Confidence: 100% (4/4 instances) ‚Üí Valid!
3

Patterns must be Novel

Novel means new knowledge that is surprising, unexpected, or counterintuitive.

üéØ This is where subjective measures matter: User's belief and domain context are crucial.
TV Scheduling Discovery
Pattern: "Programs after a UK soap opera lose 6% audience share"
Why Novel: Counterintuitive! People expect audience to increase after popular shows.
4

Patterns must be Potentially Useful

A pattern is only valuable if it can support decisions or actions.

‚ö° Without usefulness, a pattern = trivia
Actionable Examples
üè™ Retailer: Stocks umbrellas before predicted rain
üì∫ TV Scheduler: Avoids placing expensive shows after soap operas
üè• Hospital: Schedules more staff during flu season patterns
5

Patterns must be Understandable

Even valid and novel patterns are useless if humans cannot interpret them.

üß† Knowledge must be communicated in interpretable form: Rules, decision trees, visualizations
Understandable vs. Black Box
‚úÖ Understandable: "If Humidity = High ‚Üí Don't Play Tennis" (clear rule)
‚ùå Not Understandable: Complex neural network with 1000 hidden parameters

üéØ Key Insight: Quality Over Algorithms

The definition emphasizes: Quality of patterns matters more than which algorithm is used. Any algorithm can produce rules, but only proper evaluation makes them knowledge.

That's why interestingness criteria are essential: They ensure we discover meaningful knowledge, not just random patterns.
