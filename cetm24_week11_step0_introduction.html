Graph Theory Basics

Graph G = (V, E)

V (Vertices/Nodes): Individual proteins in the cell

E (Edges/Links): Interactions between proteins

In this undirected graph, edges represent bidirectional protein interactions without implying causation.

Hub Proteins

Proteins with many connections (high degree) are called hubs. These are often essential for cellular function and may be:

• Critical for cell survival

• Involved in multiple pathways

• Potential drug targets

• Evolutionary conserved
