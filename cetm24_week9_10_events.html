Event: A customer completes a purchase
Who (Customer)
  • Customer ID
  • Demographics (age, gender, income)
  • Loyalty program level
What (Product)
  • Product ID, category
  • Brand, supplier
  • Quantity purchased
When (Time)
  • Transaction timestamp
  • Date, weekday, season
  • Time of day
Where (Location)
  • Store branch, region, country
  • Shipping address
  • Online IP / device region
How (Channel)
  • Mobile app, website, physical store
  • Payment method
  • Device type
Why (Reason)
  • Discount or coupon applied
  • Seasonal promotion
  • Customer feedback

Product Extensions

Transaction Extensions
  • Basket size (items per order)
  • Total order value
  • Shipping method & delivery speed

üö¶ Event 2: Traffic Accident Investigation

Event: A traffic accident is reported and investigated
Who (Parties)
  • Driver ID, vehicle ID, pedestrian ID
  • Age, gender of drivers
  • License / insurance status
What (Accident)
  • Accident type (collision, pedestrian, single vehicle)
  • Vehicles involved
  • Severity (injury, fatality, property damage)
When (Time)
  • Date & time of accident
  • Day of week, time of day
  • Season / weather impact
Where (Location)
  • GPS coordinates, road name
  • Urban / rural / highway
  • Speed zone
How (Conditions)
  • Road condition (wet, icy, dry)
  • Vehicle condition (brakes, tires)
  • Traffic signal status
Why (Cause)
  • Human factor (distracted, alcohol, speeding)
  • Environmental factor (fog, rain, poor lighting)
  • Mechanical failure
üîç Extended Data
Person Extensions
  • Driver history, records
  • Insurance data
  • Medical condition (if relevant)
Vehicle Extensions
  • Make, model, year
  • Maintenance records
  • Safety rating
Accident Extensions
  • Police report ID
  • Photos, witness statements
  • Damage cost estimates
Context Extensions
  • Traffic volume data
  • Road design
  • Signage / crosswalk presence
‚ú® Cluster Dimension (Discovery)

At first, clusters are unknown. Analysis may reveal:

  • Accidents by location (intersections, highways)
  • Accidents by time (night vs rush hour)
  • Accidents by severity (minor vs fatal)
  • Accidents by cause (driver, vehicle, environment)

Direction: Clustering helps investigators find accident patterns and hotspots.
